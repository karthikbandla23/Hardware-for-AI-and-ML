
## 1. What are you trying to do?

I want to make computers perform large amounts of number calculations faster and more efficiently. 
These calculations are used in modern AI systems to process and understand data such as text and images. 
The goal is to reduce the time and energy needed to perform these operations.

---

## 2. How is it done today, and what are the limits of current practice?

Today, these calculations are mostly done on general-purpose processors like CPUs and GPUs. 
While these processors are flexible, they are not specifically designed for this type of repeated calculation. 
As a result, they consume a lot of energy and spend time moving data back and forth from memory, which slows down performance.

---

## 3. What is new in your approach and why do you think it will be successful?

The idea is to design a specialized hardware structure that is built specifically for these calculations. 
Instead of moving data frequently, the design keeps data close to where it is used and performs many calculations in parallel. 
This approach reduces unnecessary data movement and improves speed and efficiency, making it more suitable for AI workloads.
